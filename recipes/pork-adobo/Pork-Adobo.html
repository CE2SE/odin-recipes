Pork Adobo Recipe

Filipino style Pork Adobo

Adobo is a popular dish in Filipino cuisine, characterized by its savory, tangy flavors. It typically involves marinating meat—commonly chicken, pork, or beef—in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. The marinated meat is then simmered until tender, resulting in a dish that has a rich, aromatic sauce. Variations may include different ingredients like coconut milk or potatoes, and it's often served with rice. Adobo is beloved for its depth of flavor and is considered a national dish of the Philippines, reflecting the country’s culinary heritage.

Ingredients

  • o.5 grams Pork Shoulder
  • 1 Head Garlic
  • 1 sachet Oyster Sauce
  • 3 tbsp White Vinegar
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1 tsp Black peppercorn
  • 2 pcs Bay Leaf
  • 1 tbspVegetable Oil
  1. Combine pork, MAGGI Supreme Sarap, garlic, vinegar, water, peppercorn and bay leaf in a pot. Cover and simmer for 45 minutes. Strain pork and set aside. (50mins)
  2. Sauté pork and garlic in oil until light golden brown. Pour cooking liquid and simmer until sauce is thick and coating the pork. (10mins)
  3. Transfer on a serving plate and serve. (1min)
